Frequently asked questions

Over-ear or in-ear: which sounds better?

Over-ear headphones generally have larger drivers and more acoustic space, which produces wider soundstage and better bass extension. In-ear earbuds have closed the gap significantly, especially for commuters who need portability. For critical listening at a desk, over-ear wins; for active use and travel, earbuds are more practical.

What is the difference between ANC and passive noise isolation?

Passive isolation is physical: foam or silicone tips block sound by plugging the ear canal. Active noise cancellation (ANC) uses microphones and a processor to generate an opposite sound wave, cancelling low-frequency noise like engine hum and HVAC. Good earbuds do both; over-ear headphones rely more on ANC.

What audio codecs matter for wireless headphones?

AAC works well on iPhone. LDAC (Sony, Android) delivers higher-quality audio over Bluetooth. aptX and aptX HD are Android-side alternatives. For most listeners, the difference between codecs is smaller than the difference between headphone quality. SBC is the lowest-quality baseline fallback all Bluetooth devices support.

How long should headphone battery last?

For earbuds: 6-10 hours per charge with 24-40 hours from the case. For over-ear: 20-30 hours. ANC drains battery faster. A 6-hour earbud can realistically cover a full workday if you dock them at lunch; a 30-hour over-ear gets through a week of commuting.

Do expensive headphones always sound better?

Not linearly. The biggest jump in sound quality happens between $20 and $80 headphones. Above $150-200, improvements become subtler and more dependent on personal preference. At $300+, you're paying for premium materials, ANC quality, brand ecosystem integration, and build longevity as much as raw sound.
