Frequently asked questions

What makes headphones good for calls?

Microphone placement and background noise suppression matter most. Headphones with beam-forming microphones (multiple mics targeting your voice) and AI noise suppression filter keyboard clicks, background conversation, and environmental noise from what your caller hears.

Are earbuds or over-ear headphones better for calls?

Both can work well. Over-ear headphones with boom microphones or multiple beam-forming mics often have better mic clarity. Earbuds with stem-mounted microphones (AirPods, Galaxy Buds) are closer to the mouth and pick up voice well. The software noise suppression quality matters as much as physical mic position.

Do ANC headphones help during calls?

Yes, ANC reduces background noise you hear from the other party's side, making their voice clearer. The microphone processing handles what they hear from you. Both directions benefit from the call experience on good ANC headphones.

Can I use one earbud for calls while leaving one ear open?

Most true wireless earbuds support mono mode, only the primary earbud (usually right) needs to be in. This works for brief calls and is useful for office workers who want to stay aware of their surroundings. Remove the secondary bud and leave it in the case.

What is sidetone and do I need it for calls?

Sidetone is the ability to hear your own voice in the headphones while speaking. Without it, people tend to speak too loudly because they can't hear themselves. Some headphones offer adjustable sidetone; it's a comfort feature more than a quality one, but useful for all-day call users.
