Choosing between Apple’s AirPods Pro and Samsung’s Galaxy Buds often feels like a proxy battle for your smartphone allegiance, but there’s more nuance once you dig into the design, fit, and feature set each side brings. We’ve spent time poring over owner feedback, expert round-ups, and long-term impressions to give you a clear-eyed sense of what each pair does best. No lab numbers here — just a practical, researched take on the two biggest names in wireless earbuds.

The short answer: follow your phone

For most people, the honest answer is simple: pick the earbuds that match the phone in your pocket. The AirPods Pro deliver their most polished experience — effortless pairing, fluid device switching, hands-free Siri, and the Find My network — exclusively with an iPhone. The Galaxy Buds return the favour on Samsung phones, with seamless auto-switching, 360 audio, and a setup that just works out of the box. Use either pair cross-platform and you lose a fair amount of that integration magic, which is why your phone brand is the real shortcut to a happy purchase. The deeper comparison below unpacks what else actually matters beyond that one decision.

Sound and noise cancelling

Both the AirPods Pro and Galaxy Buds handle active noise cancelling with impressive authority, effectively hushing the rumble of a train or the hum of an office. Their sound signatures are notably refined for wireless earbuds, offering clear, balanced presentation that works across genres without pushing any frequency too far forward. The real difference is a matter of listening taste: the AirPods lean slightly more neutral and airy, while the Galaxy Buds deliver a touch more warmth and bass presence. In practice, neither set dramatically outperforms the other on audio alone, so your choice will rightly come down to which phone you carry and which fit feels more secure.

Fit, comfort and controls

Fit is where personal preference really comes into play. The AirPods Pro use a short-stem design with a secure in-ear seal and rely on a pinch sensor on the stem for volume and playback, which some find more deliberate than a tap. The Galaxy Buds3 Pro take a blade-stem approach and use touch controls along the flat edge, offering a different feel that may suit those who prefer a lighter touch. Both include multiple ear-tip sizes to help you get a snug seal, so if you can, try each in a store to see which stem style and control method feels more natural for your ears and daily use.

Ecosystem features that actually matter

If you live deep inside Apple’s world with an iPhone, iPad, and Mac, the AirPods Pro make device switching feel almost invisible — you start a call on your phone, then seamlessly move to your laptop without fiddling with settings. Hands-free Siri and audio sharing with a friend’s AirPods add a practical layer that keeps you inside the ecosystem without friction. On the Samsung side, the Galaxy Buds reward Galaxy phone and tablet owners with automatic switching of their own, plus 360 audio for a more spatial experience and effortless pairing that just works. These integration perks, rather than minute differences in sound, are what will genuinely shape your daily satisfaction, so choose the buds that match the devices you already carry.

So which should you buy?

If you carry an iPhone, the AirPods Pro are the straightforward recommendation for seamless integration and consistent performance. For a Samsung Galaxy phone, the Galaxy Buds3 Pro make the most sense thanks to better codec support and feature parity. On a non-Samsung Android, the Galaxy Buds still function well, but a pair like the Sony equivalents often offer a more balanced experience across devices.
