AirPods vs Galaxy Buds

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

If you own an iPhone, stick with AirPods for seamless integration; if you have a Samsung phone, Galaxy Buds offer superior features and controls. For everyone else, pick based on your preferred fit, sound signature, and ANC performance, AirPods are more open and airy, while Galaxy Buds deliver deeper bass and better noise isolation.

Which ecosystem do you belong to?

The single biggest factor in this decision is your phone. AirPods are built exclusively for Apple’s ecosystem: they pair instantaneously with iPhones, share audio between devices via iCloud, and let you use Siri hands-free. On an Android phone, AirPods work like basic Bluetooth earbuds, no battery readout, no transparency mode shortcut, no automatic switching. Galaxy Buds, meanwhile, shine on Samsung phones. The Galaxy Wearable app gives you full control over EQ, touch gestures, and ambient sound. They also support Samsung’s own seamless device switching and can connect to your Galaxy tablet or watch without re-pairing. On an iPhone, you lose those perks and must rely on a third-party app for basic adjustments. If you use a mix of devices, say an iPhone for calls and a Windows laptop for music, neither ecosystem is perfect, but Galaxy Buds are generally more flexible with non-Samsung hardware.

How does active noise cancellation compare?

The latest AirPods Pro offer very good ANC that cancels consistent low-frequency sounds like airplane hums and air conditioners. Their open-style fit means some high-frequency noise still leaks in, which some people find less isolating than a fully sealed design. Galaxy Buds Pro and Buds2 Pro take a different approach: they seal the ear canal with silicone tips, creating a physical barrier that already blocks a lot of noise. Combined with their ANC, they tend to reduce a wider range of sounds, including chatter and clattering dishes. Reviews often describe the Galaxy Buds’ ANC as slightly more aggressive, but it varies with fit. Neither is class-leading next to over-ear headphones, but both are excellent for commuting and open offices. Your personal fit will matter more than the chip inside.

Which sounds better? Audio quality and tuning

AirPods (regular and Pro) have a balanced, relatively neutral sound that leans slightly bright. They excel with vocals and acoustic instruments, giving a sense of openness. But they lack deep bass punch, if you listen to hip-hop, EDM, or rock, you might find them a bit thin. Galaxy Buds, especially the Buds2 Pro, deliver a warmer, bass-forward sound. Their low-end is more pronounced and punchy, and they offer customizable EQ presets in the Samsung app. Treble is smooth, not harsh, and the overall tuning is crowd-pleasing for pop and modern genres. Neither is audiophile-grade, but both are very capable. Your music taste matters: prefer natural mids? AirPods. Want thump and energy? Galaxy Buds.

How long do the batteries last?

With ANC on, the AirPods Pro deliver roughly five to six hours of listening, while the regular AirPods (third generation) manage about six hours. The case adds several additional charges, bringing the total to a day or more of moderate use. Galaxy Buds2 Pro offer a similar six-hour playback with ANC active. The Galaxy Buds2 (non-Pro) actually edge ahead slightly, often reaching seven hours. Their charging cases also provide two to three full recharges. Quick charging features are comparable, ten minutes in the case gives you about an hour of playtime on both brands. In everyday use, neither will leave you stranded, but the Galaxy Buds tend to have a minor edge in battery stamina, especially at lower volumes.

Can you use them across multiple devices?

AirPods rely on Apple’s iCloud-based automatic switching. If you’re signed into the same Apple ID on an iPhone, iPad, and Mac, the AirPods will move between them as you start media or take calls. It works well but only within Apple’s world. Switching to a non-Apple device requires manual Bluetooth re-pairing. Galaxy Buds use Samsung’s own Auto Switch, which is similarly seamless between Galaxy phones, tablets, and watches. However, they also support multipoint Bluetooth on some models, meaning you can connect to two devices simultaneously and switch between them without going into settings. AirPods do not offer multipoint, which is a meaningful advantage if you juggle a phone and laptop from different brands. If you want one earbud for both work and personal devices, Galaxy Buds are more accommodating.

Which ones are more comfortable and secure?

AirPods (regular) have a universal open-fit design that sits loosely in the ear. Many people find them comfortable for all-day wear, but they don’t stay put during exercise or when talking, they can easily fall out. AirPods Pro add silicone ear tips that seal and grip better, though they still use a shorter nozzle than most competitors. Galaxy Buds all use in-ear tips and a wing-like stabilizer on the Buds2 Pro. They insert deeper and feel more locked-in, which is great for running or gym sessions. However, the deeper seal takes getting used to, and some ears find the pressure uncomfortable after a couple of hours. Comfort is personal, but if you need earbuds that won’t budge during movement, Galaxy Buds are generally more secure. For lightweight, almost-forget-theyre-there wear, regular AirPods are hard to beat.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use AirPods with a Samsung phone?

Yes, but without most smart features. You’ll get standard Bluetooth audio and microphone, but no battery level display, no on-device controls for ANC/transparency, and no Siri. The double-tap gesture may not work consistently. They still sound fine, but you’re paying premium price for basic functionality.

Can I use Galaxy Buds with an iPhone?

They pair like any Bluetooth earbuds, but you lose the Galaxy Wearable app. That means no EQ customization, no firmware updates, and no voice assistant integration beyond Apple’s generic Bluetooth profile. Calls and music work, but the experience is limited.

Which has better ANC, AirPods Pro or Galaxy Buds Pro?

On pure noise-cancelling strength, Galaxy Buds Pro (and Buds2 Pro) tend to block a wider range of frequencies due to their deeper ear seal. AirPods Pro are very good with low-end rumble but let in more mids and highs. The difference is small, and your ear shape will affect the outcome more than the hardware.

Do AirPods or Galaxy Buds have better sound quality?

It depends on your taste. AirPods sound more natural and airy, good for acoustic and vocal-forward music. Galaxy Buds have more bass and energy, suiting pop, hip-hop, and electronic. Neither is objectively better, just tuned differently.

How do I switch devices with Galaxy Buds?

If you have a Samsung phone and tablet, it happens automatically when you play audio on the new device. For non-Samsung devices, you can use the Galaxy Wearable app or manual Bluetooth pairing. Some models also support multipoint, allowing you to stay connected to two devices at once.

Which earbuds are better for phone calls?

AirPods Pro and regular AirPods are widely praised for clear call quality, especially in quiet environments, thanks to their beamforming microphones and wind reduction. Galaxy Buds2 Pro also perform well in calls, but they can sound slightly more muffled in noisy settings. Both are adequate, but AirPods have a slight edge for voice clarity.

In shortAt the end of the day, your phone brand should drive this decision. iPhone users will get the smoothest, most enjoyable experience with AirPods. Samsung phone owners will unlock the full potential of Galaxy Buds. If you’re using a different phone or just want the best value in a given sale, think about fit and sound: want a light, open-feel earbud? Go AirPods. Want a bassier, more isolating seal? Go Galaxy Buds. Either choice will serve you well, but only one will feel truly at home in your pocket.
