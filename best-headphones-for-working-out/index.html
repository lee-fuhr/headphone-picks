Picking the right headphones for the gym comes down to two essentials: a design that stays planted during burpees or sprints, and a build that shrugs off sweat without complaint. The options here have been chosen from the well-worn consensus of owners and published reviews, not from any lab tests of our own, so you can trust they are proven to hold their own when things get heavy. Each is a safe bet for keeping the music going through your most punishing sessions.

What matters most for workout headphones

When you are mid-rep or sprinting, the most important thing is that the headphones stay exactly where you put them, so look for models with wingtips, ear hooks, or a stabilising fin that actually locks the earbud in place. Sweat and rain are inevitable, so a proper IP rating gives you honest peace of mind that the internals will survive your routine without you having to baby them. Physical or touch controls that you can reliably find and press while moving are a genuine help — you do not want to fumble with your phone when you are soaked and out of breath. Good sound is still lovely, but it only matters if the headphones have held on and stayed dry by the end of the session.

Beats Fit Pro — best for the gym overall

The Beats Fit Pro earns its place as a best-all-round gym earbud largely thanks to its flexible wingtip, which locks securely into the outer ear without the need for cumbersome ear hooks, making it feel stable during everything from heavy lifting to quick sprints. Owner feedback often praises the fit for not causing pressure points over a long session, while the substantial water and sweat resistance gives you genuine peace of mind when things get gritty. The active noise cancellation is strong enough to block out clanking weights and gym chatter, and the sound is punchy and energetic without being harsh—exactly what you want for a workout playlist. On top of that, the seamless pairing with both iPhone and Android means these are a genuinely fuss-free choice for anyone who doesn’t want to be locked into a single ecosystem.

Powerbeats Pro 2 — best secure ear-hook fit

If you frequently find yourself nudging earbuds back into place between sets or during a sprint, the over-ear hooks on the Powerbeats Pro 2 are a genuine solution — they simply do not budge once on, regardless of how much you move your head or jump. Beyond the security of that wrap-around fit, the build feels robust enough to survive being tossed into a gym bag or damp towel, and the battery life holds up across multiple sessions without needing a top-up. For anyone who wants to set and forget their earbuds for the entire workout, this is a practical, hassle-free choice.

Jabra Elite 8 Active — most rugged and sweatproof

The Jabra Elite 8 Active has built a strong reputation for handling the toughest workouts, thanks to a rugged construction that resists sweat and dust far beyond typical earbuds. The grippy, secure fit stays put during high-impact movement, which owners consistently praise for eliminating the need for constant readjustment. While durability is the headline, the active noise cancellation and energetic sound profile are more than adequate for drowning out a busy gym and keeping motivation up.

JBL Endurance Peak 3 — best budget workout buds

For anyone who wants a dependable pair of workout buds without spending too much, the JBL Endurance Peak 3 has a well-earned reputation as a solid value pick. The ear-hook design locks them in place during intense movement, while their strong water resistance means they handle sweat—and even a rinse after a swim—with no trouble. Owner feedback and reviews consistently note that the sound is clear and energetic enough for gym playlists, without the fussy extras of pricier models. If you just need a no-nonsense, secure fit for sweaty sessions and don’t want to overthink your purchase, these are a safe bet.
