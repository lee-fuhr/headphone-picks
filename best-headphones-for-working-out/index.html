Frequently asked questions

What headphone features matter most for exercise?

Secure fit (ear wings or hooks), sweat resistance (IPX4 minimum), and stable wireless connection. Sound quality matters less than fit, an earbud that falls out mid-run is useless however it sounds.

Are over-ear headphones good for working out?

For low-intensity workouts (cycling, elliptical, light lifting), yes. For running or high-intensity exercise, over-ear headphones shift with head movement and get sweaty. In-ear earbuds with secure tips dominate for active use.

Should I use ANC while running outdoors?

No, running with active noise cancellation outdoors is a safety risk. You need to hear traffic and other people. Use Transparency mode instead (AirPods Pro, Sony, Bose). it passes environmental sound through the earbuds so you stay aware.

How much sweat resistance do I need?

IPX4 handles sweat and light rain, covering most gym workouts and outdoor runs in dry conditions. IPX5 or IPX7 adds protection for heavy sweaters and outdoor use in rain. No standard earbuds are waterproof enough for swimming.

Do bone conduction headphones belong in workout lists?

For running specifically, yes, bone conduction headphones sit outside the ear canal, leaving ears open for full environmental awareness. Sound quality is lower than standard earbuds, but the open-ear safety benefit is meaningful for road running.
