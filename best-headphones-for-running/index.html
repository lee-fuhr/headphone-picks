Frequently asked questions

Can I use noise-cancelling headphones while running outdoors?

You can, but it's a safety issue. Active noise cancellation blocks traffic and pedestrians, which is dangerous on roads and shared paths. Use Transparency mode on ANC earbuds, or choose earbuds without ANC for running, awareness of your environment matters more than noise isolation outdoors.

What keeps earbuds in place during running?

Proper ear tip sizing is most important. Ear wings or silicone fins provide additional mechanical anchor points. Over-ear hooks (the loop that wraps around the outer ear) are the most secure for high-bounce activities. Try all included tip sizes before concluding earbuds won't stay in.

Are earbuds or open-ear headphones better for running?

Depends on your route. In-ear earbuds with Transparency mode are versatile for both road and gym. Open-ear (bone conduction or air conduction) leave ears fully open for maximum road awareness but have lower sound quality. For road runners, open-ear is safer; for treadmill, sealed earbuds sound better.

What water resistance rating do I need for running in rain?

IPX4 handles sweat and light rain, sufficient for most runs. IPX5 provides protection against water jets, covering downpour running. IPX7 is submersion-rated, which is more than you need for running but provides extra margin.

How should I carry my phone while running with wireless earbuds?

Running belt, armband, or shorts with a phone pocket. Wireless range is typically 10 meters or more, the phone doesn't need to be in hand. Keep the phone on the same side as the primary earbud (usually right) to minimize signal interruption by the body.
