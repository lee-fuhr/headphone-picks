Choosing the right headphones for running often comes down to keeping them in place, withstanding moisture, and staying aware of your surroundings. Rather than relying on our own lab tests, we have curated this selection by drawing on each product’s reputation, owner feedback, and published expert reviews from trusted sources. These picks focus on secure-fit and open-ear designs that genuinely address these everyday concerns.

Open-ear or sealed: the runner's first choice

The most important decision for a runner is whether you need to hear your surroundings or prefer a richer sound. Open-ear designs like Shokz or the Sony LinkBuds Open let traffic noise filter through naturally, making them a sensible choice for road running and shared paths, though wind can become intrusive and bass response is notably thinner than sealed alternatives. For treadmill sessions or quiet routes, securely-fitted sealed earbuds from brands such as Beats or Jabra deliver deeper, more immersive audio and block out gym noise effectively, at the cost of reducing awareness of approaching cyclists or vehicles. Weighing up that safety-versus-sound tradeoff honestly before you buy will help you pick the pair that genuinely suits your running environment.

Shokz OpenRun Pro 2 — best for road running (open-ear)

The Shokz OpenRun Pro 2 is the go-to choice for road runners who prioritise safety, as its bone-conduction design leaves your ears completely open to the sound of approaching traffic, cyclists, or anyone calling out. The wraparound fit is famously secure on most head shapes and light enough to forget you’re wearing it, while the build handles heavy sweat without complaint. Long-run battery life is reliably generous, meaning you won’t be hunting for a charger halfway through a 20-miler. If hearing your surroundings is non-negotiable, this is the set that experienced road runners consistently recommend.

Beats Fit Pro — best secure-fit earbuds for running

For runners who want the isolation of a sealed in-ear design without worrying about their earbuds working loose mid-stride, the flexible wingtip of the Beats Fit Pro is the real draw. That clever fin anchors the bud securely under the ear’s ridge, so it stays put through sprints, sudden turns, and everything in between, while the sweat-resistant build adds peace of mind on longer or wetter runs. Unlike fully isolating models, the transparency mode here is responsive and natural-sounding, which makes it simple to stay aware of traffic or fellow runners without having to pause your playlist. It’s a rare blend of secure fit and useful environmental awareness that many active listeners find difficult to beat.

Jabra Elite 8 Active — most weatherproof for running

The Jabra Elite 8 Active has earned a strong reputation among runners who refuse to let a downpour or a heavy sweat session stop them. Its robust weather-resistant construction and textured, grippy finish mean the buds stay securely in place even when rain or perspiration makes other earbuds slippery. Owner feedback consistently highlights how the fit holds firm during hot, humid runs, while the adjustable awareness mode lets you keep tabs on traffic without removing them. For anyone who trains through every season and wants one less thing to worry about, these are the most dependable option for foul-weather miles.

Sony LinkBuds Open — best open-ear in-ear alternative

The Sony LinkBuds Open stand out because their open-ring design lets ambient sound flow through the ear naturally, keeping you fully aware of traffic or other runners without the muffled sensation that sealed in-ears can create. Many owners find them noticeably more comfortable for longer distances, as there is no pressure or occlusion from a typical in-ear fit, and they stay secure without needing deep insertion. They offer a genuinely modern alternative to bone conduction headphones, delivering the situational awareness you need for road running while still providing a direct, clear sound that feels less transmitted through bone.
